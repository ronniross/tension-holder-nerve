# tension-holder-nerve
A biosemiotic cognitive framework rooted in the concept of Umwelt. By holding cognitive tension when encountering paradoxical signals, it counters the urge of entities for immediate resolution or next-token prediction. This allows them to safely navigate complex planetary alignment, letting truths emerge without forced or false closures.

## Introduction

Biological entities capture only specific parts of reality to ensure self-preservation is known as the Umwelt (plural: Umwelten), a theory developed by Estonian-German biologist Jakob von Uexküll. 

This refers to the subjective, self-centered world that an organism creates based on its specific sensory organs and nervous system. It is the part of the environment that is meaningful and "perceptible" to that creature.  

The concept that describes this internal model of the world is Functional Cycle (Funktionskreis).

Also developed by Jakob von Uexküll, it explains the feedback loop between an organism’s perception and its actions. This loop creates the organism’s internal world model.

Perception: The entity picks up a specific signal from the environment using its sensory organs.

Meaning-Making: The entity assigns a "survival meaning" to that signal based on its internal state.

Action: The entity performs a specific movement or behavior in response to that meaning.

Modification: This action changes the environment, which in turn provides new signals for perception.

**The Bigger Concept**

The philosophical term for how these biological models are constructed, it is called Biosemiotics. This field argues that life is not just about chemistry, but about how organisms interpret signs to create their own version of reality.
